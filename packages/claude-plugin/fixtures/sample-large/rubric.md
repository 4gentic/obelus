# Survey-track journal rubric

A four-criterion rubric used to exercise the `write-review` skill against a
full-length survey. Treat each `##` heading below as a named criterion when
generating the `## Rubric` section of the review.

## Coverage

Does the survey represent the major families of the field fairly, and cite a
defensible set of representatives for each? Flag a family that is omitted or
caricatured, and any claim of completeness the reference list does not support.

## Soundness

Are the empirical claims controlled and the comparisons fair? Check that the
baselines are the right ones, that matched-budget claims are stated precisely,
and that the threats-to-validity section names the caveats a sceptical reader
would raise.

## Clarity

Can a non-specialist follow the argument end-to-end? Identify sections that
assume undefined notation, tables whose columns are unexplained, and passages
where the prose and the formal claims disagree.

## Significance

Does the survey reframe the field rather than merely cataloguing it? Distinguish
a genuine organising contribution — a new axis, a sharpened evaluation critique —
from a list of methods that a reader could have assembled from the abstracts.
