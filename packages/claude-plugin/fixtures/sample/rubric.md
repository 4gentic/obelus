# Sample journal rubric

A small four-criterion rubric used by the `write-review` skill's tests and by
the desktop / web "Copy review prompt" path. Treat each `##` heading below as a
named criterion when generating the `## Rubric` section of the review.

## Novelty

Does the paper advance the state of the art? Note any prior work that subsumes
the contribution, and any contribution the paper underclaims.

## Soundness

Are the experiments well-controlled? Are the claims supported by the evidence
shown? Flag missing baselines, unstated assumptions, or weak ablations.

## Clarity

Is the paper readable end-to-end? Identify passages that obstruct comprehension
and figures that need redesign or relabelling.

## Significance

Will the result matter to readers in this field a year from now? Distinguish
narrow technical wins from broader shifts in framing.
