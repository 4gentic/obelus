\section{Introduction}

We present a robust, scalable, and efficient protocol for negotiating agentic autonomy between a principal and an agent.
It is worth noting that recent studies show that naive autonomy leads to irreversible errors, and that this observation is widely acknowledged in the literature.
Our approach notably addresses the three patterns of failure identified by previous work.

\section{Approach}

The protocol has three phases.
First, the principal and agent jointly specify a Trust Contract.
Second, an Escalation Interceptor gates every candidate action against the contract.
Third, a Resolution Store records worked precedent and makes it queryable.

\section{Evaluation}

We ran the protocol across 12 deployment scenarios and observed a reduction in escalation rates.
